Tools Used in the Application

Leaflet API: Leaflet is an open-source JavaScript library that allows for interactive maps to be rendered on a web page. Different data and images can be added onto the maps, and it can be extended with plugins.

Open AQ Platform API: This API has historical air quality information from the previous 90 days, gathered from locations around the world. The API is frequently updated with the latest data about various air particles.

Vue Framework: Vue is a lightweight framework, whose components can be written in JavaScript. It makes use of a virtual DOM for changes, and it supports two-way data binding, which was especially important for this application.



6 Interesting Facts

1. Madrid has relatively low averages for most particles compared to other large cities in Europe, except for carbon monoxide, which is relatively high at 330.7 µg/m³.

2. Compared to Europe, the US, and China, the average particle levels in Australia are extremely low. Almost every measurement is in the "Good" level, even in the larger cities such as Sydney and Brisbane.

3. In Mumbai the level of pm25 has continually varied over the past month, consistently ranging from around 10 µg/m³ to around 50 µg/m³, with a few spikes reaching as high as 90 µg/m³, which is in the "Moderate" level.

4. We looked up worst air qualities in America and found that California ranked very high. Being curious we thought of comparing the air quality in California to a place like China, known for not having the best of air qaulities. In conclusion, America's worse air quality is relatively not so bad in comparison to a country like China

5. pm2.5 which is partical matter including combuston particles, metals and organic compounds are found to be more commonly higher in densely populated areas. We tested this through cities in New York and cities with less population density like Alaska or Texas.
